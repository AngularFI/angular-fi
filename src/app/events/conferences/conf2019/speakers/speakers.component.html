Big Hangout 2019 talk:
Frictionless breaking changes migration with `ng update` and Schematics

The JavaScript world is always moving forward, driven by change. Angular has had 4 major and 13 minor releases since the release of 2.0 in September 2016.

We all love new features, better performance, and smaller bundle sizes. However, nobody loves breaking changes. Updating to newer versions can take time.

But, in this very moment, everyone's favorite Angular CLI walks on the stage! We can use the `ng update` command to automatically apply the required changes.

In this session, we will answer the questions:

  • How does ng update work under the hood?
  • What are these schematics things everyone is talking about?
  • How to write schematics for automated updates in your Angular libraries?

Big Hangout 2019 talk:
Digital accessibility as a Mindset

Technology has impacted every aspect of our lives today. It is very important to provide the same experience for everyone - including people with disabilities.

However, accessibility is about making digital experiences inclusive of more people including ourselves, our parents, people who are not native in a particular language and more. For example, captioning, voice recognition and responsiveness assist people with disabilities, but we are all using them.

And there is a strong business case for accessibility. Companies which keep accessibility in mind are more likely to be more innovative, profitable, have more positive branding experience and cut legal risks.

Most of the basics of accessibility are fairly easy to implement with some HTML knowledge and by following accessibility guidelines. Angular and most popular JavaScript frameworks to have accessibility embedded in the core or provide specific linters and plugins to help developers achieve inclusivity and business benefits quickly.

Finding the Right Path from AngularJS to Angular

Around half the Angular code in the world is AngularJS. Many of us have been asked - or will be asked - to upgrade an application to modern Angular. Knowing the steps is part of the story: upgrading components, services, handling third-party dependencies and other AngularJS entities.

Now there is an increasing number of valid paths to upgrade. Choosing the right one has real implications for the time and resources you need for your upgrade, and for the developer experience.

In his talk, Sam Julien will walk you through some key decision points and confidently past the point of no return.
